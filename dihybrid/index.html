In this activity, we will be investigating two genes in the fruit fly, Drosophila melanogaster. The first gene, ebony (e), controls body coloration, and the second gene, apterous (a), determines wing development.

When expressed in the homozygous recessive genotype, the recessive allele (e) for the ebony gene results in an ebony-colored body phenotype.

When expressed in the homozygous recessive genotype, the recessive allele (a) for the apterous gene results in a wingless phenotype.
